Overview

Cobalt is a hybrid instrument using both additive and subtractive techniques.  The signal sources include 6 simple FM carrier/modulator pairs,
2 tunable noise sources and a 'buzz' pulse generator. These signals are mixed and sent through a dual low-pass and band-pass filter.  The signal
is next passed through a clipping block before procedding to the effects and final output stage.


FM Operators
 Top

The first 6 "operators" are simple FM carrier/modulator pairs. Output amplitude may be modulated by LFO1, Velocity, Pressure, or MIDI
controllers CCA and CCB.  Both carrier and modulator share the same envelope but a lag time may be applied to the  modulator envelope.  If the
modulator Env slider is set to 0, the modulator has a fixed amplitude. As the Env slider increases the envelope has increassing effect over the
modulator amplitude.

Both carrier  and modulator share the same keyscale breakpoint  but  may be scaled by different amounts.   The keyscale is in db/octave.

    Envelopes

Each operator has it's own 5-stage envelope which may be configured as either an AADSR (tow attack stages) or ADDSR (two decay stages) .

The operator overview boxes allow quick preview of the states of  the 8 operators. Parameters include Carrier frequency as "Freq", Modulator frequency and Bias as "FM" and "Bias". 
Operator amplitude is indicated by sliding marks on vertical scales. The Mute button is used to mute selected operator.



Noise
 Top

Cobalt has two noise sources which may function as semi-tonal elements. The noise signals are formed by ring-modulating a sine wave with band-pass filtered white-noise.
The result is a cluster of partials around the sine wave frequency. With deviation from the center pitch is controlled by the filter band-width. Fotr narrow band-widths (~10) the
resulting sound has a definite pitch but with an airy quality.  As the band-width increases the sound becomes incresingly white-noise like. 

The two noise sources share a common envelope, key scaling and amplitude modulation. The envelope applied to noise 2 may have a lag time applied.





Buzz
 Top

The Buzz* Operator produces the first n harmonics with equal amplitude.  Using the envelope to change the harmonic count produces interesting results.

* About the name: Many synthesis languages have a "buzz" generator, Suppercollider uses the name 'blip' for the same functinality.


Filters
 Top

The filter section uses a low-pass and band-pass filter in parrallel.  These two filters track the same values but the bandpass
center frequency may be offset from the low-pass cutoff. Both filters share a dedicated ADSR envelope. The band-pass
lag slider smooths out the envelope as it is applied to the band pass filter. 

Filter output is routed through a clipping block prior to entering the effects section. The clipper is such that its output
never exceedes unity. Increased clipping effect is acheived by bossting the clipper's input gain.  The clipper gain
may be modulated by either MIDI controller CCA or CCB.



 Pitch & Vibrato
 Top

The editor Vibrato page contains miscilaneous controls mostly relaed to pitch and vibrato.

Portamento set keyboard portamento time which may also be modified by MIDI controller CC5

Vibrato uses a dedicated LFO. The bleed control sets the minimum vibrato signal I.E. The amount of vibrato
when the modwheel is at 0. Vibrato depth may be modified by MIDI pressure or controller CCA.

LFO 1 may be selectivly applied to operator amplitude. The amplitude of LFO 1 may be modified by  either
MIDI pressure or controller CCA.

The Pitch Envelope has four stages with levels set by L0, L1, L2 and L3. The time intervals between each
stage are set by T1, T2 and T3.  Level values have a range of +/- 1.0 which may be scaled by the scale factor
or by MIDI controller CC9



 Effects and Main Out
 Top

The effects stage consit of two delay lines, each with a maximum delay of 2 seconds, dedicated envelope and two
dedicated LFOs. The delay time, amplitude and pan position of both delay lines may be freely modified by
the envelope generator or the LFOs. Each delay line has FB and XFB sliders. The FB slider is used for feedback
of a delay to it's self. The XFB slider sets the amount of feedback from the other delay.


In the Mian Out section the overall amplitude in db and the dry signal mix may bve set. The velocity slider sets
velocity sensitivity for the overall amplitude, CC7 sets how much effect MIDI volume (7) has on the output
volume. If the CC7 is slider is at 7 MIDI volume is ignored. If the slider is at 1 the volume will change
from full off to full on over the range of controller 7.
