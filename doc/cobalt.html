Overview

Cobalt is a hybrid instrument using both additive and subtractive techniques.  The signal begins with 6 simple FM carrier/modulator pairs, two tunable noise sources and a 'buzz' generator. These signals are mixed and sent through a dual low and band pass filter.  The signal is next passed through a clipping block before proceeding to the effects and final output stage.



FM Operators
 Top

The first 6 "operators" are simple FM carrier/modulator pairs. Output amplitude may be modulated by LFO1, Velocity, Pressure, or MIDI controllers CCA and CCB.  Both carrier and modulator share the same envelope but a lag time may be applied to the  modulator envelope.  If the modulator Env slider is set to 0, it has a fixed amplitude. As the Env slider increases the envelope has increasing effect over the modulator amplitude.

Both carrier  and modulator share the same keyscale breakpoint  but  may be scaled by different amounts.   The keyscale is in db/octave.

Envelopes

Each operator has it's own 5-stage envelope which may be configured either as an AADSR (two attack stages) or ADDSR (two decay stages) .

The operator overview boxes allow quick preview of the states of  the 8 operators. Parameters include Carrier frequency as "Freq", Modulator frequency and Bias as "FM" and "Bias".   Amplitude is indicated by sliding marks on vertical scales. The Mute buttons is used to mute the corresponding operator.


Noise
 Top

Cobalt has two noise sources which may function as semi-tonal elements. The noise signals are formed by ring-modulating a sine wave with band-pass filtered white-noise.  The result is a cluster of partials around the sine wave frequency.  Deviation from the center pitch is controlled by the filter band-width. For narrow band-widths (~10) the resulting sound has a definite pitch with an airy quality.  As the band-width increases the sound becomes increasingly noisy.

The two noise sources share a common envelope, key scaling and amplitude modulation. The noise 2 envelope may have a lag time applied.



Buzz
 Top

The Buzz* Operator produces the n harmonics with equal amplitude.  Using the envelope to change harmonic count produces interesting results.

* About the name: Many synthesis languages have a "buzz" generator, Suppercollider uses the name 'blip' for the same functionality.


Filters
 Top

The filter section consist of a low-pass and band-pass filter in parallel.  These two filters track the same values but the bandpass center frequency may be offset from the low-pass cutoff. Both filters share a dedicated ADSR envelope. The band-pass
lag slider smooths out the envelope as it is applied to the band pass filter. 

Filter output is routed through a clipping block prior to entering the effects section. The clipper is such that its output never exceeds unity. Increased clipping effect is achieved by boosting the clipper's input gain.  The clipper gain may be modulated by either MIDI controller CCA or CCB.




 Pitch & Vibrato
 Top

The Vibrato page contains miscellaneous controls mostly related to pitch and vibrato.

Portamento sets portamento time which may also be modified by MIDI controller CC5

Vibrato uses a dedicated LFO. The bleed control sets the minimum vibrato signal I.E. The amount of vibrato when the modwheel is at 0. Vibrato depth may be modified by MIDI pressure or controller CCA.

LFO 1 may be selectively applied to operator amplitude. The amplitude of LFO 1 may be modified by either MIDI pressure or controller CCA.

The Pitch Envelope has four stages with levels set by L0, L1, L2 and L3. The time intervals between each stage are set by T1, T2 and T3.  Level values have a range of +/- 1.0 which may be scaled by the scale factor  or by MIDI controller CC9




 Effects and Main Out
 Top

The effects stage consist of two delay lines, each with a maximum delay of 2 seconds, dedicated envelope and two dedicated LFOs. The delay time, amplitude and pan position of both delay lines may be freely modified by the envelope generator or the LFOs. Each delay line has FB and XFB sliders. The FB slider is used for feedback of a delay to it's self. The XFB slider sets the amount of feedback from the other delay.


In the Main Out section the overall amplitude in db and the dry signal mix may be set. The velocity slider sets velocity sensitivity for the overall amplitude, CC7 sets how much effect MIDI volume (7) has on the output volume. If the CC7 is slider is at 0 MIDI volume is ignored. If the slider is at 1 the volume will change from full off to full on over the range of controller 7.
