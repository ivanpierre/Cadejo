Alias is a mono "matrix" synth with extensive modulation possibilities. Audio block components include three oscillators, a noise source, ring-modulator, two filters with wave shapers, pitch-shifter, flanger and two delay lines.

Virtually every major parameter may be modulated by one of 8 modulation buses. The modulation sources include three LFOs, two step-counters, two frequency-dividers, low-frequency noise source, sample-and-hold, and several MIDI signals.

Oscillators


Each of the three oscillators produce a different set of waveforms. in all other respects they are identical.

  • OSC1 - Sawtooth with sync
  • OSC2 - Variable width pulse
  • OSC3 - FM Feedback

Filters

The oscillators, noise and ring-modulator may be selectively routed to one of two filters.

Filter 1 is continuously variable between low, low*high, high and band pass modes. The low*high is produced by ring-modulating the low and high pass signals.

Filter 2 is a simulated Moog lowpass filter. The editor controls for both filters are identical with exception of the filter 1 mode slider.

Both filters has a dedicated distortion device at their inputs. The distortion algorithm is different for each filter but in both cases the clipped signal never exceeds unity.

Matrix


The matrix provides signal routing between several control sources and the audio block components. The 8 output buses, labeled 'A' through 'H', are each assigned two source signals. The two signals on an output bus are multiplied.

As a practical illustration consider mod wheel control of vibrato. Alias does not automatically provided for this common situation but it is easy to establish using the matrix. In the illustration bellow the two assignments for bus A are LFO1 and CCA. Looking at the legend CCA defaults to MIDI controller 1 - the mod wheel. As the mod wheel moves the CCA value changes between 0.0 and 1.0. This value is multiplied by the current value of LFO1 and then placed on bus A where it may be used for vibrato.

Two of the control sources are constant; 'con' and 'off'. The value of con is 1.0 while the value of off is 0. Assigning any output bus to off is equivalent to multiplication by 0 effectively turning the bus off.

Audio rate components (osc1, osc2, osc3, noise, ringmod, filter1, filter2, pitch-shifter, flanger, delay1 and delay2) may connect to any of the 8 output buses 'A' through 'H'.

Most control components (LFO1, LFO2, LFO3, LF-Noise, Sample-and-Hold, Counter1, Counter2, Divider1 and Divider2) connect to other control components directly without using buses A through H.

Low Frequency Control Signals


Alias provides 3 identical LFO's, a Low Frequency Noise source and Sample-and-Hold.

To set fixed LFO parameters assign one of the buses to "ONE", then use either the numeric frequency display or wave slider to set the desired value. If a non-constant signal is selected the given parameter is modulated with the depth determined by the display/slider value.

The LF-Noise source provides a low frequency random value. The single parameter sets the approximate rate of change.

The Sample-and-Hold samples an input source at a constant rate and holds the sampled value until the next sample period. Any one of the control sources may serve as input. A common use is to sample a noise signal to provide random values. Sampling a periodic signal, such as one of the LFOs will produce a sequence of repeating values. The exact nature of this sequence depends on the relative frequency between the LFO the sample rate and the LFO waveform.

Counters and Dividers


The control block includes two step-counters and two frequency divider cascades. The step counters produce stair-step waveforms. Each counter samples an input source and increments on positive zero crossing of the source. Once the counter reaches the maximum it starts over at the minimum value. A second input may be used to reset the counter on positive zero crossing of some other source. On reset the counter jumps to the reset value and continues counting from there. The bias value adds a fixed offset to the counter output which is then multiplied by the scale factor.

The two frequency dividers are permanently connected to LFO3. Divider1 provides odd divisions (1,3,5 and 7) while divider2 has even divisions (2,4,6 and 8). Within a divider the 4 signals are mixed to provide a wide array of complex periodic signals. The PW slider sets the relative pulse width of the individual signals before they are summed. The bias and scale parameters have the same usage as with the step counters. Divider3 is a sum of the Divider1 and Divider2 outputs.

Envelopes


The Alias control block contains three identical 5-stage envelopes. Env3 is permanently connected as the main output envelope. Envelopes 1 and 2 have no fixed usage. Using the zoom buttons the envelope curves may be magnified to any level. There is no upper limit to envelope duration.
