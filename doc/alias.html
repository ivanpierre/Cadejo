Alias is a mono "matrix" synth with extensive modulation possibilities.  Audio blocks include three oscillators, noise, ring-modulator, and two filters.
Each filter has an integrated distortion device.  Effects include pitch-shifter, flanger and two  delay lines.

Virtually every control parameter may be modulated by one of 8 modulation buses. The modulation sources include
three envelopes, three LFOs, two step-counters, two frequency divider lines,  low-frequency noise source, sample-and-hold, keyboard,
velocity, pressure, and 4 MIDI controllers.

Filters
 Top

Each of oscillator, the noise source and ring-modulator may be selectively routed down two filter paths.

Filter 1 is multi-modal with continuously variable contour between low, low*high, high and bandpass modes.  The low*high mode is
produced by ring-modulating the low and high pass signals. Each filter has a dedicated distortion device on it's input. The
distortion algorithm is different for the two filters but in both cases the clipped signal never exceeds unity.

Filter 2 is a low-pass only. The editor controls for the two filters are identical with exception of the filter 1 mode slider.




 Effects
 Top

For effects Alias provides a pitch-shifter, flanger and two delay lines. All effects are connected in parallel with the delay lines providing stereo outs.
Several of the effect parameters are controlled by the matrix buses.



Mixer
 Top

The Mixer page combines all amplitude and pan controls in one location. Other then the Main out controls all other controls are duplicated on the
relevant editor page.




Control Matrix
 Top

The matrix provides signal routing between several control sources and the audio block components. The 8 output buses are labeled 'A' through 'H'
and may each be assigned to 2 source signals.  The combined signals on an output bus are multiplied.

As a practical illustration consider mod wheel control of vibrato. Alias does not automatically provided for this common situation but it is easy
to set up using the matrix. In the illustration bellow we see the two assignments for output bus A are LFO1 and CCA.  Looking at the legend
CCA defaults to MIDI controller 1 - the mod wheel. As the mod wheel moves the CCA value changes between 0.0 and 1.0.  This value is
multiplied by the current value of LFO1 and then placed on bus A where it may be used for vibrato.  

Two of the control sources are constant; 'one' and 'off'. Assigning any output bus to 'off' is equivalent to multiplication by 0, effectively
turning the bus off. 


Audio rate components (osc1, osc2, osc3, noise, ringmod, filter1, filter2, pitch-shifter, flanger, delay1 and delay2) may connect to any
of the 8 output buses A through H.

The following control rate components LFO1, LFO2, LFO3, LF-Noise, Sample-and-Hold, Counter1, Counter2, Divider1 and Divider2  connect
to other control components directly without using buses A through H.

It is possible to establish feedback paths within the control block (say LFO1 controls frequency of LFO2 which controls frequency of LFO1) .
Such feedback paths are not really supported; they will produce results just not what you might expect.


LFOs, LF-Noise, Sample-and-Hold
 Top

Alias provides 3 identical LFO's,  Low Frequency Noise and Sample-and-Hold.

To set fixed LFO parameters assign one of the buses to "ONE", then use either the numeric frequency display or wave slider to set the desired value.

The LF-Noise source provides a low frequency random value. The single parameter sets the approximate rate of change.

The Sample-and-Hold samples an input source at a constant rate and holds the sampled value until the next sample period. Any one of the control
sources may serve as input. A common use is to sample a noise signal to provide random values. Sampling a periodic signal, such as one of the LFOs
will produce a sequence of repeating values. The exact nature of this sequence depends on the relative frequency between the LFO and the
sample rate as well as the LFO waveform.




Counters and Dividers
 Top

There are two step counters which may be used for stair-step waveforms. Each counter samples an input source and increments on positive zero crossing of the source.
Once the counter reaches the maximum value it starts over at the minimum value.  A second input may be used to reset the counter, again on zero crossing of the
input source. On reset the counter jumps to the reset value and continues counting from there. The bias value adds a fixed offset to the counter output which is then
multiplied by the scale factor.


There are two frequency dividers permanently connected to LFO3. Divider1 provides odd divisions (1,3,5 and 7)  while divider2 has even divisions (2,4,6 and 8).
Within a divider the 4 signals are mixed to provide a wide array of complex period signals. The PW slider sets the relative pulse width of the individual
signals before they are summed. The bias and scale parameters have the same usage as with the step counters. Divider3 is a sum of the Divider1 and Divider2 outputs.




Envelops
 Top

The Alias control block contains three identical 5-stage envelopes. Env3 is permanently connected as the main output envelope. Envelopes 1 and 2 have no fixed usage.

By use of the zoom buttons there is no upper limit to the length of envelope segments.
