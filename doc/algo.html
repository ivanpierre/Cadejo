ALGO is an 8-operator FM synthesizer with a fixed algorithm.

The algorithm may be divided into three sections:

  • Operators 1, 2 and 3 form a serial stack.
  • Operators 4, 5 and 6 form a duel modulator stack with feedback between operators 4 and 6.
  • Operators 7 and 8 form a serial stack with feedback on operator 8.
The feedback between operators 4 and 6 is more complex then that of operator 8 and tends to become chaotic quicker.

Released in 1983 the Yamaha DX7 is one of the most successful synths ever. The use of FM was a radical departure from the prevailing subtractive synths of the day. Yamaha introduced the terms "operator" and "algorithm" to help describe this revolutionary instrument.

  • An "Operator" is essentially an oscillator and envelope generator combined into a single unit. An operator may either be a carrier or modulator
  • In terms of FM "algorithm" describes how operators are connected together. Most FM synths allow the algorithm to be changed. For ALGO the algorithm is fixed.

Operators

ALGO's 8 operators are divided into 3 carriers (1, 4 and 7) and 5 modulators (2, 3, 5, 6 and 8).
With exception of feedback parameters for operators 6 and 8 the parameters for all operators are identical.


  • Freq - Relative operator frequency

  • Bias - Fixed value added to frequency. A bias may be used in at least two ways.

    • Bias values greater then about |20| introduce an effect reminiscent of Ring Modulation.
      Operators may be placed in a fixed frequency mode by setting Freq=0 and a non-zero bias value.

    • Chorusing. It is often more desirable to introduce chorusing by a small bias value (say less then 10)
      then by using a frequency detune. This is because a bias offset has a fixed beat frequency across the
      keyboard while a detuned frequency does not. For example assume a bias value of 0.0 and a detune of 1.018
      At 220Hz the beat frequency is about 4hz. Two octaves higher at 880Hz the beat frequency is about to 16Hz.
      As a counter example assume a bias value of 4.0 and a detune of 1.000. At both 220 and 880 Hz the beat
      frequency will be 4 Hz.

  • Amplitude - Operator amplitude may be modulated by several sources. Negative modulation depths
    decrease operator amplitude.

    • Vel - Velocity
    • Prss - Pressure
    • LFO1
    • LFO2
    • CCA - MIDI controller A
    • CCB - MIDI controller B

  • Keyscale - Operator amplitude may be scaled by key number. It is not apparent on the drawing above but
    there are 2 handles on the keyscale slider, one each for left and right break-points. The break-points
    set the key-number where the scaling effect takes effect. The left and right depth values set the polarity
    and steepness of the scaling curve in db/octave. There are two typical use cases for scaling modulators.
    On the right a negative scale factor is often useful to roll back harsh upper harmonics. A right-hand
    negative scale is also useful for taming feedback on operator 6.

    A positive left-hand key scale is often useful to brighten up bass timbres.

envelopes


ALGO has 9 identical ADDSR envelopes, one each for the 8 operators and a global 'Pitch' envelope.
Each envelope has an attack stage, duel decay stages, a sustain stage, and once the key has been
lifted a final release stage. The editor provides preset 'gate', 'percussion' and 'ADSR' curves,
random curve generation and cut and paste between envelopes. The envelope graph may be zoomed in or
out to any level. The horizontal guide lines stop at about 100 seconds but the display may be zoomed out
past this point. There are no hard limits for maximum length of envelope stages.

Feedback

The feedback parameters for operators 6 and 8 are nearly identical. The only difference is that for OP6 LFO1 may modulate feedback amount while for OP8 LFO2 is used.

The feedback on OP6 is more complex then that on OP8. As a practical matter this means that OP6 feedback
is harder to control and more chaotic then OP8.

  • FB - Fixed feedback depth
  • Env - Envelope modulation of feedback
  • LFO1/LFO2 - LFO modulation of feedback
  • Prss - Pressure
  • CCA - MIDI controller A
  • CCB - MIDI controller B

The HP slider sets the cutoff frequency of a high-pass filter used to block any DC component in the feedback signal.
Normally this value should be set to the maximum possible but also below any expected frequency. At cutoff values below
about 10HZ the filter takes a few milliseconds to stabilize after the input signal has changed. This may be exploited to
introduce a blip into the sound

Operator Overview and Mute


At the bottom of each operator editor is an overview of the major parameters for all operators. The values shown
are operator frequency, bias, amplitude and mute status. Muted operators do not contribute to the final signal.

LFO's

ALGO has two general purpose LFOs. A third LFO is dedicated to vibrato and found on the pitch page. LFO1 and LFO2 are nearly identical. They both produce continuously variable waveforms between positive-saw, triangle and negative-saw. Frequency, amplitude and wave shape may be modulated by various sources. The only difference is a slight difference of modulation sources. For LFO1 the pitch envelope (labeled as "ENV1") may be used to modulate amplitude or waver shape, and for LFO2 the modulation source is LFO1

  • Frequency

    • CCA - MIDI controller A
    • CCB - MIDI controller B

  • Amplitude

    • ENV1 / LFO1
    • Prss - Pressure
    • CCA - MIDI controller A
    • CCB - MIDI controller B

  • Wave

    • Skew - Wave shape
    • ENV1 / LFO1

Pitch



The editor Pitch page contains three elements

  • Vibrato

    • Frequency
    • Sens - sensitivity
    • Depth - minimum depth
    • Delay - onset time

  • Pitch

    • Port - portamento time
    • Env - pitch envelope mod depth
    • LFO1 - LFO1 pitch mod depth
    • LFO2 - LFO2 pitch mod depth

  • Pitch Envelope

Effects

Algo effects consist of a duel delay line and reverb

  • Delay

    • Delay 1 - delay time (1 sec max)
    • Delay 2 - delay time (1 sec mac)
    • Feedback - common delay feedback
    • Damp - common high frequency damping of feedback
    • Mix - wet signal mix

  • Reverb

    • Size - room size
    • Mix - wet signal mix

  • Out

    • LP - Fixed low pass filter cutoff
    • CC7 - MIDI volume controller depth
    • Amp - output amplitude in db
