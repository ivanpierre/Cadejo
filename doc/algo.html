ALGO is an 8-operator FM synthesizer. Two of the operators provide FM feedback. Additional components include 3 LFOs, a pitch envelope, a dual delay line and reverb.



The FM 'Algorithm' may be divided into three sections corresponding to the three carrier operators  1, 4 and 7.

Operators 1, 2 and 3 form a serial stack,

Operators 4, 5 and 6 form a single carrier with dual modulators. This stack provides feedback between operators 4 and 6. The nature of this feedback is complex and often hard to predict.

Operators 7 and 8 form a simple carrier/modulator pair with more traditional feedback for operator 8.


With minor variations, mostly due to feedback, the parameters for each operator are identical.



OP Freq - Sets frequency of operator relative to played pitch. If the note is A440 and freq is 2.0 the operator will produce a tone at 880 Hz.

OP Bias - Adds a fixed value to the operator frequency. There are a couple of applications for the bias value.
First if the operator frequency is set to 0 the bias value is  used to set a fixed frequency. Using a fixed frequency operator can produce sounds
reminiscent of ring modulation. 

Small bias values may be used to introduce chorusing. This is often more desirable then detuning the frequency
parameter because the chorus beat frequency is static with a bias value. With a detuned frequency the beat rate increases with higher notes.

OP Amplitude - For the carrier operators 1, 4, and 7, the amplitude parameter sets the relative (linear) mix of the operator.  For modulators
the amplitude sets the modulation index. The sliders immediately below the amplitude display provide amplitude modulation from various sources.

OP Keyscale - Sets the amount of amplitude scaling relative to a key breakpoint. It is not obvious on the drawing above but the keyscale slider
has two heads, one each for left and right breakpoints. The scale values are in db/octave. For example if the right breakpoint is at key 72 and the
right scale value is at -9, then the operator amplitude will be down -9 db by key 84 and -18 db at key 96.  A little negative key scaling on modulators
may be used to roll back harmonics, which often become harsh,  on upper keys, The same technique may be used to tame OP 6 feedback on higher
notes.  Conversely modulator keyscale boosting is often useful on the bass end to produce richer bass timbres.

OP Overview and Mute - The boxes at the bottom of the editor provide a quick overview of the frequency and amplitude of all operators  without
switching editor pages. These boxes are also used to mute operators. The operator mute states are saved as part of the program data.



OP Feedback - As noted above there are two feedback paths on operators 6 and 8. The operator 6 path is more complex and becomes chaotic
faster then operator 8. The controls for OP6 and OP8 feedback are nearly identical. The only difference is that OP6 may be modulated by LFO1
while OP8 may be modulated by LFO2. The feedback parameter are as follows:


 FB - Fixed feedback depth

Env - Amount of op envelope applied to feedback depth

LFO1/LFO2 - Amount of LFO applied to feedback depth

Prss - Amount of MIDI pressure applied to feedback depth

CCA - Amount of MIDI controller A applied to feedback depth

CCB - Amount of MIDI controller B applied to feedback depth

HP - Fixed high pass filter cutoff, for value below approx 10 Hz a blip component may be produced.




Envelopes



Each Operator has a dedicated ADDSR envelope. The pitch envelope has the same overall format.

The zoom buttons zoom in to and out of the origin and may be set to any level magnification.  The y-axis at the origin will drift a bit with increasing zoom levels but the editor status line always displays the location of the mouse is it moves over the envelope graph. You may also zoom out to any level  which means there is no practical upper bounds to envelope segment times. At around 130 seconds the horizontal guide lines come to an end but the graph may be zoomed out beyond this point. 





LFOs

ALGO has three LFOs:  LFO1, LFO2 and Vibrato.

LFO 1 and 2 are nearly identical with the only difference being the modulation sources available. The vibrato LFO is found on the Pitch page and is simpler then LFO 1 and 2.

The frequency, amplitude and wave of LFOs 1 and 2 may be modulated by various source.  CCA and CCB are MIDI controllers, Prss is MIDI channel pressure.  ENV1 is in reference to the pitch envelope and not the envelope for OP1.  Basically the difference between LFO1 and LFO2 is that LFO1 may be modulated by ENV1 while LFO2 may be modulated by LFO1.




Pitch
The controls on the editor pitch page are fairly simple. You may set the vibrato frequency, sensitivity, depth and delay.
In the pitch panel are controls for portamento time and pitch modulation depth from the pitch envelope ,LFO1 and LFO2.




Effects
ALGO uses a dual delay line and reverb for effects. The delay time may be set independently up to 1 second for the two delays.
The delays share feedback, HF damp, and mix level parameters.

Reverb room size and mix are adjustable.

The "Out" panel controllers overall amplitude. The LP slider is controls the cutoff frequency of  a fixed low pass filter at ALGO's
output, The CC& slider controls how much effect MIDI controller 7 (Volume) has on overall amplitude.
