 Multiplexed Acoustical Systems of America was an organ manufacture in Philip K Dicks We Can Build You. 
With the market for organs drying up MASA switched to the manufacture of Abraham Lincoln simulacra.... 


MASA is an organ loosely based on the Hammond B3. The tone consist of 9 sine wave partials each with a possible 8 amplitude values. By default MASA uses the same frequency gamut as the B3 (Actually not quite. The B3 partials were tuned to equal tempered instead of just ratios. The 2nd MASA partial is at 1.5 while the B3 was closer to 1.498. For technical reasons there were a few other harmonic deviations in the upper registers.) 




Unlike the fixed frequency gamut of the B3, MASA partials may be tuned to any value. The final three rows of buttons are used to set partial frequencies. The 9 "F Edit" buttons allow the frequency of each partial to be set individually. The
row of 4 buttons below this set various preset gamuts. Buttons on the final row randomize the frequencies in various ways.

Klstr

Detune
Randomly detune 1 or 2 partials.
NOTE: There is an editor bug which does not update the displayed partial frequencies after one of these  8 buttons is clicked.
Effects





The scanner is an attempt to emulate the unique "post vibrato" scanner device of the Hammond.  It consist of 8 delays in series which are selected or "scanned" in a cyclical manner. To even out the abrupt jump at the end of a scanning cycle opposing delay outputs are cross-wired.
