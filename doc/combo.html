Combo was the first Cadejo instrument and serves as a test bed for further development.
There were two design goals:

  1. It was to be a practical instrument.
  2. It was to be as simple as possible given condition 1.

The tone consist of 4 elements (1,2,3, & 4). Elements 1 and 2 produce square/pulse waves,
elements 3 and 4 produce sine, FM "sawtooth" and FM noise. With the coarse and fine detune
sliders at 0 the frequency relationship between the elements is 1:2:3:4. With the coarse slider
set to maximum the frequency relationship becomes 1:3:4:5.

Both relative mix and wave shape may be adjusted for each element. The tone may further be
modified by application of the filter. The filter has two parameters; curve and harmonic.
The available curves are: lowpass, bandpass, highpass, notch and bypass (filter off).
The filer cutoff/center frequency tracks the keyboard at a fixed harmonic from the fundamental.

The vibrato is a sine wave with rate and sensitivity controls.

Reverb has a fixed delay-time, the only control is wet signal mix

The flanger rate and feedback values are set moving the 'x' around the 2D field.
