Cadejo Manual









Heaven




Combo is a simple electronic organ. Tones are created by mixing 4 elements (numbered 1 through 4). Each element has an associated mix and wave slider.
Elements 1 and 2 produces waves ranging between square and relatively narrow pulses. Elements 3 and 4 use FM feedback and produce tones ranging between sine waves
through FM 'sawtooth'  and  FM noise.

With the coarse and fine detune sliders at 0 with frequency relationship between the 4 elements is 1:2:3:4. With the detune slider at 1.0 the frequency relationship becomes
1:3:4:5, Intermediate positions produce non-harmonic tones.  Then fine detune slider may be used to introduce a chorusing effect.

The tone may be further modified by the  key-tracking filter.  The filter modes are: Bypass, Lowpass,  Highpass,  Bandpass  and  Notch. 

Clicking the number box below the filter curve sets the filter cutoff/center
in terms of harmonic number.  For example if the key A440 is played
and the filter is set to the 4th harmonic the cutoff/center will be at
1760 Hz.


Vibrato is introduced by the Modulation wheel and the two vibrato sliders are rate and sensitivity. The vibrato rate has a range approx  0.17 to 7 Hz.

The Flanger has four controls, Rate, Feedback, Depth and Mix. The
Rate and Feedback values are set by moving the 'x' handle within the
rectangular field.

The Reverb sets the level of the wet reverb signal. The reverb decay time is fixed.

All amplitude and mix sliders have linear responses.
